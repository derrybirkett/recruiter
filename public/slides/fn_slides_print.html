01 / 10 Brief
FounderNest · Design Case · Candidate Search
Search for the way
recruiters actually think.
A typed description and structured filters, working as one. Clear about what's applied, why a candidate appears, and how to change it.

Problem framing

  • Prompt-only search is vague; filter-only search is tedious.
  • Recruiters don't know what belongs where — and the two often conflict.
  • Geography is hierarchical, not a free-text field.
  • "Why this candidate?" has to be answered on the card, not in a modal.

Key assumptions

  • Recruiters work on desktop, keyboard-first, all day.
  • The prompt extractor is imperfect — every inferred attribute is editable.
  • One mental model: prompt = intent, filters = rules. Filters always win.
  • List + detail is enough. No maps, no charts.

Design principles

  • One applied state. One source of truth, always visible.
  • Provenance. Every chip shows where it came from (prompt vs. you).
  • Conflicts are respectful. We surface, you decide. Never silently override.
  • Iteration over correction. Loosen, tighten, relax — in one click.

What "good" looks like

  • A recruiter pastes a prompt and lands on a tight result set in under 10 seconds.
  • They can scan what's applied without scrolling or hunting.
  • When something's off, the UI tells them why — before they ask.
  • Zero results never feels like a dead end.
